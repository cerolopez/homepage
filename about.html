About

Hi, I'm Cecilia

I'm a professional with 10+ years of experience in editorial and website management, currently in the process of transitioning to a career in CS after earning a Master's degree in Computer Science from Northeastern University, Silicon Valley.

My primary areas of interest include HCI, web dev and design, and UX/UI. I'm secondarily interested in AI, NLP, and large language models.

My Journey to CS

During my undergrad, I remember being drawn to computer science, but being intimidated by the math. It didn't come naturally to me and I didn't have the confidence to keep at it. I decided instead to pursue a Bachelor's in Linguistics, another passion of mine. After graduating in 2014, I found a job as a Content Editor and since then have been working in similar roles.

In 2019, I was excited to learn that you could earn a Master's in Computer Science without a background in CS through the Northeastern Align Program. Being surrounded by such brilliant research scientists and engineers at Meta (where I worked back then), I was inspired to go for it. In August 2021, I decided to apply at the last minute, got accepted about a week later, and enrolled the next month.

For about a year, I continued to work full-time at Meta while in grad school full-time. However, my time at Meta came to an unexpected end when I was let go in the first round of layoffs at the end of 2021. Although I was devastated, I took this opportunity to focus my full attention on finishing school. I graduated in May 2024 and since then have been working part-time so I can focus on shifting careers.

I am looking forward to inspiring the next generation of engineers the same way that my colleagues at Meta inspired me. In particular, I'm passionate about showing other folks underrepresented in tech that if I can do it, they can do it, too.
